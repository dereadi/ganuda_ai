## 1. Market Overview

### 1.1 The AI Governance Gap

The AI industry in 2026 is defined by a structural contradiction: organizations are deploying AI systems with increasing autonomy while having no standardized mechanism for governing those systems. The gap between AI capability and AI oversight is widening.

**Key market signals:**
- EU AI Act enforcement began in stages (2024-2026), creating compliance urgency
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) adopted as de facto US standard
- OMB M-24-10 mandates AI governance for federal agencies
- SEC issuing guidance on AI use in financial services
- HIPAA enforcement actions increasing for AI-related data incidents

## 2. Competitive Landscape

### 2.1 Direct Competitors

**There are currently no direct competitors offering a compiled governance proxy with mandatory adversarial dissent.** The closest categories are:

| Category | Examples | What They Do | What They Don't Do |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **AI Safety / Alignment** | Anthropic (Constitutional AI), OpenAI (RLHF) | Train models to be "more aligned" | Structural governance. Same model judges itself. |
| **AI Observability** | Arize, WhyLabs, Weights & Biases | Monitor model performance and drift | Govern model behavior. No pre/post inference checks. |
| **AI Guardrails** | Guardrails AI, NeMo Guardrails (NVIDIA) | Input/output filtering rules | Adversarial dissent. Democratic consensus. Audit trail. |
| **MLOps / Governance** | MLflow, Domino, DataRobot | Model lifecycle management | Real-time inference governance. Constitutional framework. |
| **Compliance Platforms** | OneTrust, TrustArc, BigID | Privacy/compliance management | AI-specific governance. Model-level oversight. |

### 2.3 Key Insight

The market currently treats AI governance as either:
- **A training problem** (make the model better) — Anthropic, OpenAI
- **A filtering problem** (block bad inputs/outputs) — Guardrails AI, NeMo
- **A monitoring problem** (observe and alert) — Arize, WhyLabs

**Ganuda treats governance as a constitutional problem** — separation of powers, mandatory dissent, democratic consensus, graduated autonomy. This is a fundamentally different approach that creates a new category.

## 5. Pricing Strategy

### 5.1 Principles

1. **Free at the bottom, paid at the top.** Community tier is genuinely free (Apache 2.0). Revenue comes from organizations that get business value.
2. **Per-node, not per-seat.** AI governance scales with infrastructure, not headcount.
3. **PolyForm protects communities.** Free for individuals, tribal entities, nonprofits, education, and businesses under $5M revenue.
