## CRITICAL: Medication Impact Rule (LIVE — 38 CFR 4.10)

**Federal Register: 2026-03068, RIN 2900-AS49**
**Effective: February 17, 2026 (IMMEDIATE)**
**Comment period: Open until April 20, 2026**

- Disability ratings now based on function WHILE ON medication, not unmedicated baseline
- Overrides CAVC ruling in *Ingram v. Collins* (March 2025) which required "baseline severity" without medication
- VA Secretary Douglas Collins signed Feb 11, 2026
- Affects ALL body systems — musculoskeletal, mental health, cardiovascular, digestive
- Applies to new claims AND existing disabilities up for reevaluation
- 350,000+ pending claims affected
- DAV, VFW, Combat Veterans of America all opposing

## Proposed Rating Changes (Expected Late 2026)

### Tinnitus (DC 6260) — ELIMINATION PROPOSED
- DC 6260 would be eliminated. Tinnitus folded into DC 6100 as symptom.
- Currently most common VA claim. 10% standalone rating would disappear.

### Sleep Apnea (DC 6847) — RESTRUCTURING PROPOSED
- CPAP use alone no longer guarantees 50%
- New ratings: 0%, 10%, 50%, 100% (30% eliminated)
- Based on: treatment effectiveness, comorbidities, end-organ damage

### Mental Health — DOMAIN-BASED SYSTEM PROPOSED
- Five measurable domains replace subjective "occupational and social impairment"
- Domains: Cognition, Interpersonal Interactions, Task Completion, Navigating Environments, Self-Care
- 0% rating eliminated (minimum 10% for service-connected diagnosis)
- Grandfathering applies to existing ratings
