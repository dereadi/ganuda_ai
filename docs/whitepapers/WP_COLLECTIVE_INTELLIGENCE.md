## **5. Weighted Consensus Voting**  
The 7-Specialist Council operates on a **weighted consensus voting system**, where each specialist’s vote carries a weight determined by their expertise, historical accuracy, and relevance to the issue at hand. This mechanism ensures that decisions are both informed and equitable.  

### **5.1 How Weighted Voting Works**  
1. **Weight Assignment**: Each specialist is assigned a weight based on:  
   - **Expertise**: Depth of knowledge in the relevant domain.  
   - **Performance**: Accuracy of past recommendations.  
   - **Relevance**: Direct impact of the specialist’s role on the issue.  

   For example, in a decision about cybersecurity (SECURITY), Eagle Eye’s weight would be higher than that of Turtle.  

2. **Voting Process**: Specialists cast votes on proposed actions. Votes are multiplied by their assigned weights.  
3. **Threshold for Consensus**: A decision requires a minimum weighted score (e.g., 70% of total possible weight) to pass.

## **7. Confidence Scoring**  
To enhance decision quality, the system assigns **confidence scores** to each specialist’s input. These scores are derived from historical data, peer reviews, and performance metrics.  

### **7.1 Confidence Score Calculation**  
The score is calculated using the formula:  
```
Confidence Score = (Accuracy × Relevance × Timeliness) / 3  
```  
- **Accuracy**: How often the specialist’s past recommendations were correct.  
- **Relevance**: How closely their expertise aligns with the current issue.  
- **Timeliness**: How quickly they respond to new data.  

### **7.2 Application in Decision-Making**  
- Specialists with higher confidence scores have greater influence in the weighted voting process.  
- Low-confidence scores trigger additional scrutiny or consultation with other specialists.

## **8. Audit Hashes for Traceability**  
To maintain transparency and accountability, every decision made by the 7-Specialist Council is recorded with an **audit hash**. These hashes are cryptographic hashes (e.g., SHA-256) that uniquely identify the state of the system at the time of the decision.  

### **8.1 How Audit Hashes Work**  
1. **Generation**: When a decision is finalized, the system generates a hash of the input data, voting records, and final outcome.  
2. **Storage**: Hashes are stored in a decentralized, immutable ledger.  
3. **Retrieval**: Stakeholders can access past decisions by querying the ledger using the hash.  

### **8.2 Benefits**  
- **Transparency**: All decisions are verifiable.  
- **Accountability**: Any misuse of the system can be traced back to specific actions.  
- **Historical Learning**: Past audits inform future improvements.
