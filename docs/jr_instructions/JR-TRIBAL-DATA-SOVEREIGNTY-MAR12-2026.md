## Objective

Research tribal data sovereignty legal frameworks and sovereign cloud market landscape to determine whether Ganuda's federation architecture has a natural fit as a tribal sovereign cloud platform.

## Scope

### Otter (Legal) Research
1. Cherokee Nation Data Sovereignty Task Force -- Executive Order 2024-07-CTH, objectives, recommendations, AI policy outcome
2. CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance -- Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics
3. OCAP framework (Canada) -- Ownership, Control, Access, Possession
4. Tribal Digital Sovereignty legal definition as formalized by NCAI/AIPI
5. Federal infrastructure -- BIA Indigenous Connectivity and Technology Division
6. Environmental/legal risks of data center hosting on tribal lands

### Deer (Market) Research
1. Competitive landscape -- Arctic IT, Handel IT, Data Holdings, Microsoft, Animikii
2. Market size indicators -- $3B TBCP grants, 574 federally recognized tribes, proxy TAM estimates
3. Institutional infrastructure -- Center for Tribal Digital Sovereignty (CTDS), TribalNet conference
4. Partnership opportunities -- NCAI, Arctic IT, Data Holdings, Cherokee Nation, BIA, Ford Foundation/Google.org

## Deliverable

Research document: `/ganuda/docs/research/DEER-OTTER-TRIBAL-DATA-SOVEREIGNTY.md`

## Key Findings Summary

1. **Cherokee Nation has active data sovereignty policy** -- Task Force delivered recommendations, Chief Hoskin signed first tribal AI governance policy
2. **CARE principles are the legal standard** -- people-and-purpose-oriented, complement FAIR data principles
3. **Forest County Potawatomi built first tribal data center** -- $33M, on trust land, hosts internet exchange
4. **CTDS is the institutional hub** -- NCAI + ASU, Ford/Google funded, coalition model
5. **No governance-topology sovereign cloud exists** -- gap in market between infrastructure (Arctic IT/Microsoft) and policy (CTDS/NCAI)
6. **Ganuda's architecture maps naturally** -- council governance, federation, CARE alignment, Cherokee scaffolding
