## Core claims from the video

1. **Federal Reserve is initiating crisis-level protocols** around private credit exposure. Fed examiners are asking major US banks about their direct and indirect exposure to private credit funds, following a surge in redemptions and rise in troubled loans.
2. **Banks are launching credit default swaps against their own lending exposure.** JP Morgan + S&P Global preparing an index called **CDX Financials** (or "Finex index") — credit default swaps that protect buyers against defaults by private credit managers. Van Metre frames this as the same playbook as 2008. More than **$3 trillion industry** in private credit, and the new derivatives let hedge funds short the space with leverage.
3. **Banks tightening C&I loan standards** while Fed deregulation is simultaneously trying to make banks more willing to lend to private credit. Van Metre reads this as banks knowing the quality problem and pulling back while the Fed is still trying to expand access.
4. **PIK debt ("payment in kind")** is masking underlying defaults. Instead of making loan payments, borrowers tack missed payments + interest + penalties onto the end of the loan. Van Metre: *"in a lot of cases, some of these loans, they've never made a payment."*
5. **Macro stress indicators**:
   - CPI surged **0.9% monthly** (largest since 2022), driven by energy
   - Core CPI only 0.2% — consumers already trading down
   - **UMich consumer sentiment 47.6** (preliminary April), down from 53.3 — described as record-low range
   - Pawn shop loan demand up (discretionary stress reaching working-class Americans)
   - Weekly hours of production/non-supervisory employees: still holding, but Van Metre calls this the leading indicator — *"the moment we see that drop, everything starts to fall apart"*
6. **Fed will cut rates when it blows up.** Banks have been buying treasuries in anticipation of this — Van Metre reads treasury-buying behavior as bank-side positioning for a rate cut.
7. **"Wall of looming debt maturity"**: $200B+ of high-yield and leveraged loan tech debt maturing through 2028. When these hit refinance, they won't be able to refinance.
8. **Retail sales haven't dropped yet but sentiment has** — Van Metre's argument is that retail typically follows sentiment with a lag, and if retail follows now, *"game over for the financial system."*

## What Van Metre is wrong about (for Council's calibration)

- **Timing.** Perma-bearish shops have predicted "one month to collapse" consistently since ~2020. Van Metre's specific timing has missed multiple times.
- **"Fed will cut" simplicity.** Fed response to stress is not deterministic. In some scenarios Fed cuts (2020 COVID), in others Fed pauses or even tightens through stress (1979 Volcker).
- **Video omits supply-side resilience signals** (labor market strength, household balance sheet improvements from pandemic-era debt restructuring, tax receipts holding up through Q1 2026).
- **Private credit stress ≠ systemic banking collapse.** Banks have regulatory capital buffers from Dodd-Frank that didn't exist in 2008. Private credit defaults can hurt bank profits without triggering bank runs. This is a real but not-necessarily-existential channel.

## What Van Metre is likely right about (for Council's calibration)

- **Fed asking banks about private credit exposure IS a signal.** Fed doesn't launch broad inquiries for no reason.
- **CDX Financials / Finex launch IS real** and IS analogous to 2008-era CDS innovations. Even if it doesn't trigger collapse, it signals that institutional hedgers expect stress.
- **PIK debt IS masking underlying weakness.** This is documented in S&P and Moody's corporate credit reports going back 18 months.
- **UMich sentiment at 47.6 IS historically low.** That number is real.
- **$200B+ of maturing tech debt through 2028 IS real.** The refinance wall is quantified.
- **"Maybe a month" is a reasonable operational planning horizon for a practitioner who remembers how fast 2008 moved.** Bear Stearns → Lehman was about six months from first cracks to collapse. The federation should be prepared to act inside that window.
