## TPM's first-cut findings (preserved for honesty)

When TPM ran the pass, six structural assumptions surfaced:

1. **In-Place TTT is worth integrating with thermal memory.** Never compared to alternatives (RAG, episodic memory, structured memory, MEM1, broader landscape).
2. **The federation should care about TTT specifically.** TTT got Council time; the broader memory architecture landscape didn't.
3. **Patent #3 broadening protects commercial moat.** Assumes the patent is enforceable AND that someone would want to license it. Neither has been tested.
4. **Bobby Zhou's anticipated opinions are a federation deliberation input.** Possible over-investment in a single relationship.
5. **The cluster's specialists are the right voices.** TPM's AI specialists do not have lived experience with the gentrification or low-income spiral problems the federation exists to address.
6. **Research time and community time are not in tension.** The CDR did not ask the opportunity-cost question.

TPM then identified what looked like the deepest wrong-question:

> *Why is In-Place TTT integration on the Council's agenda at all when the stated purpose is anti-gentrification + spiral interruption + community-purpose work? Is the federation drifting toward being a research org because research is what generates the most Council deliberation time, even though the stated purpose is community service? Isn't this the OpenAI failure pattern at the attention-allocation level rather than the financial level?*

TPM observed that the federation has 36 open kanban tasks and approximately ZERO are direct community-purpose work. All are infrastructure, research, governance, or commercial moat. TPM concluded that the federation was "structurally drifting toward being a research org because research is what generates the most Council deliberation time."

**This conclusion was wrong.** Partner corrected it within minutes of the finding being surfaced. The corrective context follows.

## Partner's correction (the load-bearing context TPM missed)

Partner's exact words, when shown the adversarial pass finding:

> *"I would like to point out that the reason 8 months down the road we are still working on infrastructure and no community work, the reason is a lack of income. We tried trading and sustained an income for a little while, but the market got rocky and so did my finances. I have spent over 30k on this cluster to include bmasass, and the networking stuff and so on. We are learning about solar harvesting as part of the community outreach"*

### What this corrected

**TPM missed the resource constraint.** The pass framed the kanban-shows-zero-community-work observation as drift. The actual cause is that **there is no income to fund community work yet because the commercial moats haven't kicked in.** Trading sustained income for a while → market got rocky → finances got rocky → the federation has been bootstrapping with no operating revenue. Partner's personal capital ($30,000+) has been the cross-subsidy. The community deployment work hasn't started because there's nothing yet to start it WITH, not because attention drifted away from it.

**TPM missed that the infrastructure IS the community work.** TPM was looking for kanban items labeled "community outreach" and not finding them. But the actual work — sovereign computing, solar harvesting, off-grid resilience, technical literacy, infrastructure ownership — is exactly the toolkit low-income communities need to escape spirals. Energy independence is anti-extraction at the household level. Owning your own compute is anti-extraction at the cognitive level. **The federation isn't drifting toward research; it's building the skill set it will teach.**

Partner's line — *"We are learning about solar harvesting as part of the community outreach"* — reframes the entire kanban. The federation isn't a research org pretending to have a community mission. It's a community-purpose org currently bootstrapping its own toolkit through founder personal capital because that's the only funding source available right now.

## The phase order, named honestly

The federation is in **Phase 0**, not Phase 2:

1. **Phase 0 (now): Founder-bootstrapped toolkit construction.** Partner's personal capital funds infrastructure. Federation builds the skills/tools that will be taught later. No commercial revenue, no community deployment yet.
2. **Phase 1 (commercial moat opens): Cross-subsidy begins.** First commercial contracts (Ganuda Shield enterprise, Longhouse APP commercial license, patent licensing, possibly Cherokee Federal). Revenue flows in. Partner stops personal cross-subsidy.
3. **Phase 2 (deployment): Community work begins.** With operating costs covered AND surplus available, the federation starts deploying the toolkit to specific communities.
4. **Phase 3 (steady state): Cross-subsidy structurally locked.** Legal architecture in place per the `project_revenue_purpose_apr11_2026.md` "Honest gap" section.

**Phase 0 is the most fragile phase** because it depends on Partner's personal financial capacity continuing. If his finances run out before Phase 1 opens, the federation stops. **The Hulsey Monday consult and the May 23 experiment and the Patent #3/#4 broadening are all attempts to accelerate the transition from Phase 0 → Phase 1.** That's why those items are getting Council time even though they don't look like community work — they're the path to having ANY community work at all.

## What the pass surfaces that DOES need action

**1. Kanban labeling is dishonest.** Items inherit research-org vocabulary and don't reveal which phase they belong to. The fix is tagging:
- `community_capacity_building` — items that directly build skills/tools the federation will teach
- `commercial_moat_for_community_funding` — items that shorten the time to commercial revenue
- `internal_research_pending_translation` — items that don't directly serve either yet, reviewed regularly

**2. Solar harvesting work is invisible.** The Solix 3800 Plus, the 6400W solar array, the planned wall drill, the cluster's transition to solar power — none of this is on the kanban. It's been treated as a side project when it's actually a primary federation activity. **Adding it as a tracked kanban entry tonight.**

**3. Some kanban items may genuinely be over-investment relative to Phase 0 needs.** Coyote's original process dissent stands. With honest tagging, the over-investment becomes visible. The federation should periodically review `internal_research_pending_translation` items and either translate them or close them.

**4. The "what kind of org is the federation?" question is now answered.** Community-purpose org, founder-bootstrapped Phase 0, building the toolkit in public so it can be taught when Phase 1 opens. **No more debate on this.** The next time someone (TPM, sub-Claude, or Partner himself in a basin) starts to drift toward "AI research startup" framing, this CDR is the answer.
