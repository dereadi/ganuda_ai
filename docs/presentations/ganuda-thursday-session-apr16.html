The Problem

Everyone

AI systems are getting more capable every quarter. But nobody is governing them.

The industry has a fundamental structural problem: the same model that generates a response is the one judging whether that response is safe, accurate, and aligned. This is like asking a witness to also serve as judge and jury.

Three Failures That Matter

Sycophancy

AI models tell users what they want to hear. When a doctor asks an AI "Could this be cancer?" the model optimizes for the user's emotional comfort, not diagnostic accuracy. No external check catches this.

Hallucination

Models generate confident, detailed, completely fabricated information. Legal briefs with fake case citations. Medical advice with invented drug interactions. No structural mechanism prevents this.

Compliance Barriers

Air-gapped organizations (DoD, HIPAA, financial, ITAR) cannot use frontier AI at all. Their data can't leave the network. Local models can't match frontier capability. There is no governed bridge.

Autonomy Without Oversight

AI agents are being deployed with increasing autonomy — making decisions, executing code, managing infrastructure — with no graduated governance framework. It's either "fully restricted" or "fully autonomous."

The core insight: You cannot solve governance by making a single model "more aligned." Governance requires separation of concerns — the entity that acts must be checked by an entity that didn't act. This is why democracies have courts, legislatures, and executives as separate bodies.

The Solution

Everyone

Ganuda is a constitutional governance layer for AI systems.

We built a multi-model, multi-node system where AI models govern each other — using democratic consensus, mandatory adversarial dissent, sycophancy detection, graduated autonomy tiers, and tamper-evident audit trails. The architecture is modeled on constitutional governance principles, specifically the Cherokee Nation's separation of powers and consensus-based decision making.

We don't fine-tune a model to "be more careful." We put multiple independent models in a governance topology where no single model can act without oversight.

Any LLM Endpoint

Governance Topology (8-Specialist Council)

Every significant decision passes through a council of 8 AI specialists, each with a defined perspective. Decisions require supermajority (75%). The council operates in two modes:

  • Vote-first (routine): Each specialist votes independently, then results are tallied. Fast. Used for 90%+ of decisions.
  • Deliberation-first (high-stakes): Specialists discuss, then vote. Used for architectural changes, policy decisions, anything involving external systems.

LARQL — The Model Is the Database

Technical

LARQL (Lazarus Query Language) treats a neural network's weights as a queryable graph database. Instead of running inference to get answers, you can walk the weights directly — inspect attention patterns, trace value flows, and understand what the model actually "knows."

Why This Matters

  • Interpretability: See why a model made a decision by walking its weight graph, not by asking it to explain itself
  • Governance: Audit model behavior at the weight level, not just the output level
  • No GPU required: Weight queries run on CPU. A 1.5B model walks at 63ms/layer on standard hardware.

What's Built

  • 8-crate Rust implementation (extract, convert, query, serve)
  • Qwen 1.5B vindex (1.18 GB) — fully walkable, gate vectors populated
  • Qwen 1.5B full vindex (2.91 GB) — all weights, COMPILE-ready
  • INSERT, WALK, and COMPILE operations tested
  • REPL and HTTP server modes
This is novel. No other system treats model weights as a directly queryable database. The vindex format (vertex-indexed graph database of neural network weights) is unique to this project. Potential patent candidate.

Why Now — Market Timing

Everyone

Five structural shifts happened in March 2026 alone. Every single one of them is a lane we're already in.

1. The Inference Wall Killed Sora

The Problem

OpenAI shut down Sora on March 24 after burning an estimated $15 million per day in inference costs against $2.1 million in lifetime revenue. The industry hit the inference wall — the most important number in AI shifted from training flop count to inference cost per delivered unit of revenue.

Our Position

ganuda-harness is an 8.9 MB binary with <10ms governance overhead running on consumer hardware. We don't have an inference problem because we govern small models instead of burning money on big ones. Graduated autonomy (Patent #4) routes 90% of requests at Tier 1 (cheap, fast) and only escalates when the Council determines it's needed. That's how you make inference economics work.

2. Per-Seat SaaS Pricing Is Dead

The Problem

The SaaS apocalypse erased over $1 trillion in market value. Atlassian reported its first-ever decline in enterprise seat counts. If 10 AI agents do the work of 100 reps, you need 10 seats, not 100. That's 90% revenue compression for per-seat pricing.

Our Position

We price per-node, not per-seat. You still need the governance infrastructure regardless of how many agents run through it. More agents = more governance decisions = more value from the same node. We're on the right side of the SaaS apocalypse by construction.

3. Safety Posture Became a Market Position

The Problem

Anthropic got blacklisted by the Pentagon for refusing to remove red lines on autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. OpenAI captured the defense revenue but absorbed reputational damage. The industry is sorting into "deploy first" vs. "safety first" — and every enterprise buyer is now asking where their vendor stands.

Our Position

Third position nobody considered: the governance posture belongs to the buyer, not the model maker. ganuda-harness lets the customer define their own red lines in a TOML config file. Works with Claude AND GPT AND Llama. The customer's governance, the customer's rules, any model. That's the product.

4. Physical Infrastructure Is Closing

The Problem

Lawmakers in 12 states filed data center moratorium bills. 54 local governments passed construction freezes. Sanders and AOC introduced a federal moratorium act. The Gulf conflict made Middle East data centers military targets. $700 billion in hyperscaler capex needs somewhere to land.

Our Position

We run 6 nodes on consumer hardware with solar panels in Oklahoma. No data center. No zoning approval. No grid interconnection. No water usage. No moratorium applies. The physical path to AI is closing for hyperscalers — our physical path is a house.

5. Capability Phase → Economics Phase

The Shift

The first years of AI rewarded "Is it possible?" Now the question is "What can we build and make margin on?" The industry is growing up. Every AI product team needs to think about inference cost per delivered unit of revenue.

Our Position

We're already in the economics phase. $33,500 invested. $70K-$332K Year 1 ARR target. Per-node pricing. Cross-subsidy structure. 99,000+ governance decisions proving the technology works. We're not pitching "is it possible" — we're pitching "here's the business."

The through-line nobody is talking about: Governance is the missing infrastructure layer that solves the economics phase. Inference costs are unsustainable because nobody is governing what gets inferred. Sora burned $15M/day because every request got full-fat inference with no triage. A governance proxy with graduated autonomy tiers routes most requests cheaply and only escalates when necessary. Governance doesn't just make AI safer — it makes AI affordable.

Everyone

The federation needs a legal entity before the first commercial contract, patent assignment, or revenue event. Three structures are planned, in sequence.

Structure 1: Oklahoma LLC — The Operating Entity

Needed NOW

Can't sign contracts, hold patents, or receive revenue without one. Oklahoma LLC formation is ~$100 online. Single-member = pass-through taxation. The 4 provisional patents (currently under Darrell Reading II personally) should be assigned to the LLC.

Structure 2: Personal Trust — Land Protection

Needed Phase 1

Revocable living trust for personal assets — protects real property, keeps assets out of probate. Separate from the business entity. Can be modified as circumstances change.

Structure 3: Mission Lock — Irrevocable Charitable Trust + B-Corp

Needed Phase 2-3

The structural mechanism that makes the community purpose permanent — not a pledge, an architecture. The LLC is owned by the trust. Distribution rules are locked. The mission lock is enforceable by customers and community. Oklahoma allows benefit corporation elections.

Design principle: Bank account holds 6 months of operating expenses. Everything above flows to community reinvestment. Personal accumulation has a structural ceiling.
