## How to Recognize You're on a Fence (Not a Tree)

### Signs You're Climbing a Fence:
1. **It hurts** (barbed wire is cutting you, but you ignore pain)
2. **It goes nowhere** (fence doesn't have branches, fruit, shelter)
3. **It's isolated** (fence separates you from others, tree connects to forest)
4. **It's artificial** (humans built it recently, not evolved over millennia)
5. **It requires endless climbing** (tree has places to rest, fence demands constant ascent)
6. **Speed is mandatory** (must keep moving or fall, tree allows stillness)
7. **It serves the builder** (fence benefits whoever erected it, tree benefits ecosystem)

### Signs You're Climbing a Tree:
1. **It nourishes** (tree gives fruit, shade, oxygen)
2. **It connects** (tree is part of forest, mycelium network, ecosystem)
3. **It has rhythm** (tree has seasons, dormancy, cycles)
4. **It evolved with you** (your species co-evolved with trees over millions of years)
5. **It allows rest** (branches are for sitting, not just climbing)
6. **Slowness is okay** (turtle wisdom - slow and steady)
7. **It serves all relations** (Mitakuye Oyasin - tree serves birds, insects, fungi, soil, air, future seeds)
